Customer Journey Mapping for EV Charging Networks

Introduction:
EV charging networks are growing rapidly, and understanding the customer experience is crucial for their success. This report explores the customer journey mapping for EV charging networks, highlighting the key touchpoints, emotions, and behaviors that customers exhibit during their interaction with these networks.

Methodology:
This study is based on qualitative research conducted from April to July 2021, exploring the motivations for purchase and the customer experience for electric vehicle (EV) owners from consideration through to use, with a key focus on understanding the customer experience in relation to charging.

Results:
1. Consideration: The customer journey begins with consideration, where potential customers research and evaluate different EV models and charging options.
2. Purchase: Once the customer has decided to purchase an EV, they will research and compare different charging networks and plans.
3. Charging: The customer will then use the charging network, which may involve finding charging stations, paying for charging, and monitoring their charging status.
4. Post-Charging: After charging, the customer may receive notifications and updates about their charging status and may need to troubleshoot any issues that arise.

Touchpoints:
1. EV manufacturer websites with charging information
2. Car dealership education sessions
3. Online forums (Reddit r/electricvehicles, Tesla Motors Club)
4. Charging apps (PlugShare, ChargePoint, ABRP)
5. Social media EV communities
6. Friends and family testimonials
7. YouTube review videos
8. Government EV incentive programs
9. Utility company EV programs
10. Environmental organization resources

Emotions:
- Anxiety: About charging availability and range limitations
- Excitement: About environmental benefits and technology
- Confusion: About charging standards (CCS, CHAdeMO, Type 2, Tesla)
- Concern: About costs and infrastructure readiness
- Optimism: About future transportation solutions
- Overwhelm: From information overload and conflicting opinions

Behaviors:
- Researching charging locations along commute routes
- Calculating total ownership costs including charging
- Testing different charging apps and planners
- Visiting public stations to observe usage
- Consulting current EV owners about experiences
- Comparing home charging installation requirements
- Evaluating workplace charging availability
- Researching network coverage in travel areas
- Assessing charging speed requirements
- Investigating payment methods and subscriptions

Conclusion:
Understanding the customer journey for EV charging networks is essential for improving the overall customer experience. By identifying the key touchpoints, emotions, and behaviors that customers exhibit during their interaction with these networks, EV charging network providers can optimize their services to meet the needs of their customers.

Recommendations:
1. Improve charging infrastructure and availability
2. Simplify charging standards and payment methods
3. Enhance customer education and support
4. Invest in digital platforms for seamless charging experiences
5. Foster partnerships with EV manufacturers and other stakeholders to promote interoperability and convenience
