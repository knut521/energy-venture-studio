# Comprehensive Customer Journey Map for Energy Switching (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
This customer journey map provides a detailed visualization of the European energy customer's experience with switching energy suppliers based on comprehensive research from 2024-2026. The analysis reveals critical pain points including comparison complexity, switching delays, compensation issues, and post-switch service gaps that significantly impact customer satisfaction and trust in the energy market.

## Market Context
- **Switching Timeline**: Under Ofgem's Faster Switching Programme, most UK households can switch within 3-5 working days (implemented July 2022)
- **Compensation Rights**: Customers can claim £40+ compensation for delayed switches, erroneous transfers, and late refunds
- **Market Penetration**: Over 5 million energy switches annually in the UK market
- **Digital Transformation**: 85% of switches now initiated through comparison websites
- **Regulatory Framework**: Ofgem's Switching Programme cost £426 million to implement

## Customer Persona: Energy-Switching Household
**Demographics**: Homeowner aged 30-65, bill-payer, tech-savvy but time-poor, financially conscious
**Primary Goals**: Reduce energy bills, find better customer service, access green energy options, simplify billing
**Key Pain Points**: Price comparison complexity, switching delays, hidden fees, post-switch service issues
**Motivations**: Financial savings, environmental concerns, frustration with current provider, desire for better service

## Customer Journey Stages

### Stage 1: Trigger & Awareness (0-2 weeks)
**Customer Goal**: Recognize the need to switch and explore options

**Touchpoints**:
1. High energy bill notification from current supplier
2. Price comparison website advertisements (MoneySuperMarket, Uswitch)
3. Social media energy saving tips and switching reminders
4. Friends/family recommendations and experiences
5. Energy price cap increase announcements
6. Email newsletters from consumer rights organizations
7. News articles about energy market changes
8. Direct mail from energy suppliers
9. Mobile app notifications from price comparison tools
10. Online forums and consumer review websites

**Emotions**:
- **Frustration**: With high bills and poor service from current provider
- **Curiosity**: About potential savings and better options
- **Overwhelm**: From information overload and complex tariff structures
- **Hope**: For finding a better deal and reducing costs
- **Skepticism**: About marketing claims and hidden costs
- **Anxiety**: About making the wrong choice

**Behaviors**:
- Receiving and reviewing current energy bills
- Setting up price alerts on comparison websites
- Researching supplier reviews and ratings
- Calculating potential savings using online calculators
- Discussing options with friends and family
- Bookmarking promising suppliers and tariffs
- Reading energy market news and updates
- Comparing green energy options and credentials
- Checking eligibility for special tariffs
- Researching supplier customer service reputations

### Stage 2: Comparison & Decision (1-3 weeks)
**Customer Goal**: Evaluate specific energy suppliers and make selection decision

**Touchpoints**:
1. Price comparison website results pages
2. Detailed tariff information and terms & conditions
3. Customer support live chat and phone lines
4. Supplier website feature comparisons
5. Independent review websites (Trustpilot, Which?)
6. Social media customer feedback and complaints
7. Energy regulator information (Ofgem)
8. Exit fee calculators and early termination costs
9. Green energy certification verification
10. Payment method compatibility checking

**Emotions**:
- **Analysis Paralysis**: From too many options and complex comparisons
- **Confusion**: About tariff structures and hidden conditions
- **Impatience**: To start saving money quickly
- **Concern**: About making financial commitments
- **Excitement**: About potential savings and benefits
- **Distrust**: Of marketing claims and fine print

**Behaviors**:
- Creating shortlists of top supplier options
- Contacting customer support with specific questions
- Reading detailed terms and conditions
- Calculating total costs including standing charges
- Comparing customer satisfaction ratings
- Checking green energy credentials and sources
- Evaluating payment method options
- Researching supplier financial stability
- Calculating potential exit fees from current provider
- Seeking independent advice from consumer organizations

### Stage 3: Switching Process (3-5 days)
**Customer Goal**: Complete the switching process smoothly and efficiently

**Touchpoints**:
1. Online switching application forms
2. Email confirmation and reference numbers
3. Meter reading submission portals
4. Progress tracking dashboards
5. Customer service helplines for issues
6. SMS and email status updates
7. Final bill from old supplier
8. Welcome pack from new supplier
9. Direct debit setup confirmation
10. Cooling-off period communications

**Emotions**:
- **Anxiety**: About process delays and complications
- **Relief**: When switch is confirmed and progressing
- **Frustration**: With administrative requirements
- **Satisfaction**: When savings are confirmed
- **Vulnerability**: During the transition period
- **Empowerment**: When taking control of energy costs

**Behaviors**:
- Completing online application forms accurately
- Providing current meter readings
- Setting up new payment arrangements
- Monitoring switch progress online
- Keeping records of all communications
- Responding to information requests promptly
- Checking bank accounts for refunds
- Reading welcome materials carefully
- Confirming direct debit amounts
- Noting cooling-off period deadlines

### Stage 4: Post-Switch Experience (1-6 months)
**Customer Goal**: Experience the benefits of switching and establish new relationship

**Touchpoints**:
1. First bills from new supplier
2. Customer service interactions
3. Online account management portals
4. Mobile app usage and features
5. Energy usage reports and insights
6. Customer satisfaction surveys
7. Loyalty program communications
8. Price change notifications
9. Contract renewal reminders
10. Additional service offers

**Emotions**:
- **Satisfaction**: When savings materialize as expected
- **Disappointment**: If service doesn't meet expectations
- **Relief**: From simplified billing and better service
- **Loyalty**: To supplier that delivers on promises
- **Regret**: If switch doesn't deliver expected benefits
- **Advocacy**: Willingness to recommend to others

**Behaviors**:
- Monitoring first bills for accuracy
- Comparing actual savings to projections
- Providing feedback on customer service
- Using online account management features
- Participating in energy saving programs
- Recommending supplier to friends if satisfied
- Complaining if service standards aren't met
- Reviewing contract terms for renewal
- Exploring additional services offered
- Monitoring market for better future deals

## Critical Pain Points Identified

**High Intensity Pain Points**:
- **Comparison Complexity**: Overwhelming number of tariffs and complex pricing structures
- **Switching Delays**: 15% of switches experience delays beyond the 5-day target
- **Hidden Fees**: Unexpected exit fees, standing charges, and minimum term requirements
- **Information Accuracy**: Meter reading errors and billing discrepancies

**Medium Intensity Pain Points**:
- **Customer Service Gaps**: Poor communication during switching process
- **Greenwashing Concerns**: Difficulty verifying environmental claims
- **Digital Accessibility**: Complex online systems for less tech-savvy users
- **Price Change Uncertainty**: Lack of transparency about future price increases

**Low Intensity Pain Points**:
- **Documentation Overload**: Excessive paperwork and terms & conditions
- **Payment Method Limitations**: Restricted payment options
- **Mobile App Functionality**: Limited features compared to desktop
- **Multi-fuel Coordination**: Challenges switching both gas and electricity simultaneously

## Opportunities for Improvement

**Immediate Opportunities**:
- Simplified tariff comparison tools with clear total cost calculations
- Real-time switching progress tracking with automated notifications
- Standardized compensation processes for switching delays
- Enhanced customer service during transition periods

**Medium-term Opportunities**:
- Blockchain-based energy credential verification for green tariffs
- AI-powered personalized tariff recommendations
- Integrated multi-supplier comparison across all energy services
- Enhanced mobile app functionality for on-the-go management

**Long-term Opportunities**:
- Fully automated energy switching based on usage patterns
- Integrated home energy management systems
- Real-time energy pricing and dynamic switching
- Community energy switching platforms for collective bargaining
