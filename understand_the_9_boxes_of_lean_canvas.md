The 9 components of the Lean Canvas are:

1. Problem: Clearly define the problem you are trying to solve.

2. Customer Segments: Identify your target audience and understand their needs and pain points.

3. Unique Value Proposition: Clearly define what sets your product or service apart from others.

4. Solution: Describe how your product or service solves the problem for your customer segment.

5. Channels: Outline the marketing and sales channels you will use to reach your customer segment.

6. Revenue Streams: Describe how you will generate revenue from your product or service.

7. Cost Structure: Outline the costs associated with running your business.

8. Key Metrics: Identify the metrics that will be used to measure the success of your business.

9. Unfair Advantage: Describe what sets your business apart from others and makes it unique.

Example of GOOD output:

Problem: Homeowners struggle to reduce their energy bills.

Customer Segments: Homeowners aged 25-55 living in urban areas.

Unique Value Proposition: Our product offers a simple and effective way to reduce energy bills.

Solution: Our product uses advanced technology to optimize energy usage.

Channels: Social media, online advertising, and partnerships with energy companies.

Revenue Streams: Subscription fees and advertising revenue.

Cost Structure: Development costs, marketing expenses, and operational costs.

Key Metrics: Customer acquisition cost, customer retention rate, and revenue growth.

Unfair Advantage: Our team of experts in energy efficiency and technology.

Example of BAD output:

Problem: Unknown.

Customer Segments: Unknown.

Unique Value Proposition: Unknown.

Solution: Unknown.

Channels: Unknown.

Revenue Streams: Unknown.

Cost Structure: Unknown.

Key Metrics: Unknown.

Unfair Advantage: Unknown.

Quality Criteria:

* Minimum word count per deliverable: 500 words

* Required data points: At least 5 components filled with valid data

* Structural requirements: All components must be filled, and each component must have at least 2-3 sentences

Frameworks & References:

* Lean Canvas: A framework for creating a business model canvas

* Business Model Generation: A book by Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur that provides a comprehensive guide to business model innovation

* Customer Development: A methodology for customer development and validation

Common Mistakes:

* Failing to clearly define the problem and customer segment

* Not providing a unique value proposition

* Failing to describe the solution and channels

* Not identifying revenue streams and cost structure

* Not setting key metrics and unfair advantage
