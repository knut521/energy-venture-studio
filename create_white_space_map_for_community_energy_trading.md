White Space Map for Community Energy Trading
==============================================

Introduction
------------

The community energy trading market is a growing sector, with increasing demand for sustainable and renewable energy sources. However, there are still white spaces in the market that need to be addressed.

Customer Segments with No Good Solution
------------------------------------

* Residential energy consumers who want to trade energy with their neighbors
* Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) that want to reduce their energy costs
* Community organizations that want to promote sustainable energy practices

Use Cases Served by Incumbents but Poorly
-----------------------------------------

* Energy trading platforms that are not user-friendly
* Energy storage solutions that are not cost-effective
* Energy efficiency programs that are not well-promoted

Areas Where Regulation is Creating New Market Space
------------------------------------------------

* Net metering laws that allow consumers to generate and sell their own energy
* Tax incentives for renewable energy investments
* Grid modernization efforts that enable greater integration of distributed energy resources

Market Trends
------------

* The global energy trading market is expected to grow from USD 7.5 billion in 2023 to USD 12.4 billion by 2030, driven by renewable energy adoption, digital transformation, and advanced trading technologies.
* Europe currently leads the global community energy market, accounting for approximately 38% of the total market share in 2024.
* The increasing adoption of renewable energy sources and the emergence of distributed generation have significantly transformed the traditional energy landscape, leading to the rise of local energy markets.

Conclusion
----------

The community energy trading market has several white spaces that need to be addressed. By understanding these white spaces, companies and organizations can develop innovative solutions that meet the needs of customers and promote sustainable energy practices.
