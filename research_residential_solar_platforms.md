The residential solar energy market has several gaps, including the lack of standardization in energy monitoring systems, the high upfront costs of solar panel installation, and the limited consumer awareness about the benefits of energy monitoring and the available solutions. Additionally, the market is expected to fall short of the 750 GW target by 2030, with only 718 GW of installed solar capacity projected. The market is also facing challenges such as the decline in valuations, large capital injections, and major bankruptcies. To address these gaps, there is a need for more affordable and accessible solar energy solutions, as well as education and awareness campaigns to inform consumers about the benefits of energy monitoring and the available solutions.
