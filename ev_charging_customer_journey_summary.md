# EV Charging Customer Journey Map - Summary Report

## Project Overview
This project involved creating a comprehensive customer journey map for European EV charging networks based on 2024-2025 market research. The journey map visualizes the complete customer experience from pre-purchase consideration through advanced usage optimization.

## Methodology

### Research Approach
1. **Market Analysis**: Comprehensive review of global and European EV charging infrastructure data
2. **User Research**: Analysis of customer satisfaction studies, user feedback, and community discussions
3. **Pain Point Identification**: Systematic mapping of customer frustrations and challenges
4. **Touchpoint Mapping**: Detailed documentation of all customer interactions across the journey
5. **Emotional Analysis**: Tracking of customer emotions and behaviors at each stage

### Data Sources
- IEA Global EV Outlook 2024-2025
- European Alternative Fuels Observatory (EAFO)
- Industry reports from McKinsey, Roland Berger, BloombergNEF
- Customer satisfaction studies (JD Power and others)
- User community feedback and forums
- Direct customer interviews and observational data

## Key Findings

### Market Context
- **Global Market**: $40.22B in 2025, projected $238.82B by 2033 (25% CAGR)
- **European Leadership**: Netherlands (180,000+), Germany (160,000), France (155,000) charging points
- **Growth Metrics**: 8,000+ new points in Austria during 2024 alone

### Critical Pain Points
1. **Reliability Issues**: 30% failure rate across charging attempts
2. **App Fragmentation**: Requirement for 3-5 different applications
3. **Payment Complexity**: Multiple payment methods lacking standardization
4. **User Experience**: Complex interfaces and poor usability
5. **Infrastructure Gaps**: Uneven geographic distribution

### Customer Expectations
- Reliability with minimal downtime
- Single app access across networks
- Transparent pricing and availability
- Seamless payment processes
- Comprehensive geographic coverage
- Responsive customer support

## Journey Stages Mapped

### Stage 1: Awareness & Consideration (Pre-Purchase)
- **Focus**: Understanding charging requirements before EV purchase
- **Key Activities**: Research, cost calculation, app testing, consultation
- **Emotions**: Anxiety, excitement, confusion, concern, optimism

### Stage 2: Acquisition & Setup (0-3 Months)
- **Focus**: Establishing reliable charging routine
- **Key Activities**: Installation, app configuration, payment setup, testing
- **Emotions**: Frustration, confidence, anxiety, satisfaction, overwhelm

### Stage 3: Routine Usage (3-12 Months)
- **Focus**: Maintaining reliable charging access
- **Key Activities**: Regular charging, app usage, payment management, support interactions
- **Emotions**: Frustration, satisfaction, anxiety, annoyance, relief

### Stage 4: Advanced Usage & Optimization (12+ Months)
- **Focus**: Optimizing efficiency and cost effectiveness
- **Key Activities**: Smart charging, energy management, V2G exploration, community leadership
- **Emotions**: Empowerment, frustration, satisfaction, advocacy, confidence

## Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-6 Months)
1. Develop universal payment protocols
2. Create app consolidation platforms
3. Implement real-time reliability monitoring
4. Establish transparent pricing standards

### Medium-term Initiatives (6-18 Months)
1. Negotiate cross-network roaming agreements
2. Deploy predictive maintenance systems
3. Enhance user education resources
4. Improve customer support responsiveness

### Long-term Strategy (18+ Months)
1. Integrate vehicle-to-grid (V2G) capabilities
2. Develop smart charging optimization
3. Incorporate renewable energy integration
4. Execute comprehensive infrastructure expansion
