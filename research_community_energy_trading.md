Community Energy Trading Platforms:

2. Aidness - A decentralized renewable energy trading platform that enables peer-to-peer transactions of renewable energy between producers and consumers.

3. TradingView - A platform for charting, trading, and connecting with a global community of traders and investors.

4. Energy Community - A platform that enables energy communities to manage energy trading for grid stability, match demand and supply locally, and enable P2P energy trading.

Customer Pain Points:

1. High energy costs: Individuals and organizations may experience high energy costs due to the lack of access to affordable renewable energy sources.

2. Limited access to renewable energy: Some individuals and organizations may not have access to renewable energy sources, such as solar or wind power, due to geographical or financial constraints.

3. Complexity of energy trading: The process of buying and selling energy can be complex and may require significant expertise and resources.

Gaps in the Market:

1. Limited availability of community energy trading platforms: There is a need for more platforms that enable individuals and organizations to buy and sell energy with each other.

2. Limited access to energy storage solutions: There is a need for more energy storage solutions that enable individuals and organizations to store excess energy generated by renewable sources.

3. Limited availability of renewable energy sources: There is a need for more renewable energy sources, such as solar or wind power, to support the growth of community energy trading.

In conclusion, community energy trading has the potential to promote energy efficiency, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and support local economic development. However, there are several challenges and gaps in the market that need to be addressed, including the limited availability of community energy trading platforms, energy storage solutions, and renewable energy sources.
